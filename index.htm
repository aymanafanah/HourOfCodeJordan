HOUR OF CODE

Dec 8th – 14th, 2014

Hour of Code (HoC) is a global movement reaching tens of millions of students in 180+ countries. Anyone, anywhere can organize an Hour of Code event. One-hour tutorials are available in over 30 languages.
No experience needed.

Microsoft is supporting Code.org with significant financial resources and volunteer leadership because we share the goal that all kids should have the opportunity to learn computer science. Satya Nadella, Microsoft’s CEO, has announced that Microsoft will support this initiative to introduce computer science to millions of students worldwide.

Microsoft’s Role

In Jordan, Microsoft Jordan’s MIC Team was contacted by MIC Corp to drive this initiative locally. Microsoft Student Partners (MSPs) have conducted HoC sessions in both public and private schools at their campuses, and school communities.

Jordan Education Initiative (JEI)

This initiative was conducted in partnership with Jordan Education Initiative (JEI) and had enabled these sessions to be conducted in almost all the JEI schools – which constitute of 150 public schools.

Next Steps

Starting from 2015, Microsoft Jordan is planning on elevating HoC on a regular basis between students in Jordan whether from public, private school or universities, through the below steps:

  • Collaborate with a telecom sponsor for the next round of HoC
  • Join forces with JEI to have the HoC with schools on a regular basis
  • New rounds to ensure all participating schools and universities to have a stable internet connection (via telcom sponsorship)
  • Train teachers in schools and unviersities to be able to follow up with students throughout the year
  • Find a way to integrate HoC within the student curriculum through utilizing JEI interns to conduct these sessions throughout the year
  • Involve students and motivate them to participate in joining Microsoft’s Imagine Cup yearly competition and Microsoft Virtual Academy
